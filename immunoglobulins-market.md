<p><p>Immunoglobulins, also known as antibodies, are proteins produced by the immune system to help fight off infections. There are five main types of immunoglobulins: IgG, IgA, IgM, IgE, and IgD. IgG is the most abundant and provides long-term immunity. IgA is found in mucosal areas and helps prevent pathogens from entering the body. IgM is the first antibody produced in response to an infection. IgE is involved in allergic reactions. IgD's function is still being researched. These different types of immunoglobulins play a crucial role in protecting the body from harmful pathogens.</p></p>
