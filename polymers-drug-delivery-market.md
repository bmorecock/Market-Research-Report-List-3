<p><p>Polymers in drug delivery offer various types of market solutions including micelles, microgels, dendrimers, hydrogels, and other specialized systems. Micelles are self-assembled nanostructures capable of encapsulating drugs for targeted delivery. Microgels are cross-linked polymer networks that can swell in response to external stimuli, releasing drugs in a controlled manner. Dendrimers are highly branched macromolecules with defined structures for precise drug delivery. Hydrogels are 3D networks that can trap water and drugs, gradually releasing them. Other polymer systems offer unique properties for specific drug delivery applications.</p></p>
