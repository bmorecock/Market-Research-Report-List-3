<p><p>Passive dosimeters are devices used to measure an individual's exposure to radiation without the need for external power sources or active monitoring. The three main types of passive dosimeters in the market include Optically Stimulated Luminescence (OSL) Dosimeters, which use light to measure radiation exposure, Thermoluminescent Dosimeters (TLD), which measure radiation by heating the dosimeter to release stored energy, and other types of dosimeters that utilize various technologies such as film badges or pocket dosimeters. These dosimeters are essential tools for monitoring radiation exposure in industries such as healthcare, nuclear power, and manufacturing.</p></p>
