<p><p>Cephalosporins are categorized into four generations based on their spectrum of activity and effectiveness against different types of bacteria. First generation cephalosporins are effective against gram-positive bacteria, while second generation cephalosporins have increased activity against gram-negative bacteria. Third generation cephalosporins are even more effective against resistant bacteria, and fourth generation cephalosporins have a broader spectrum of activity, including against bacteria that are resistant to other antibiotics. Each generation caters to different types of infections and market needs.</p></p>
