<p><p>Aneroid Sphygmomanometers are the most common type of manual sphygmomanometers in the market. They work by using a dial gauge to measure blood pressure. On the other hand, Mercury Sphygmomanometers use a column of mercury to measure blood pressure and are known for their accuracy. While aneroid sphygmomanometers are more portable and easy to use, mercury sphygmomanometers are preferred in clinical settings for their precision. Both types of manual sphygmomanometers are essential tools for monitoring blood pressure accurately.</p></p>
