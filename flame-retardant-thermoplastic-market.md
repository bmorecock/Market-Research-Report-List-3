<p><p>Flame-retardant thermoplastic materials can be divided into two main types: amorphous polymers and crystalline polymers. Amorphous polymers have a random arrangement of molecules, making them transparent and rigid. They offer good impact resistance and are often used in applications requiring high clarity and toughness. Crystalline polymers have a more ordered molecular structure, making them more rigid and heat-resistant. They are commonly used in applications requiring high strength and temperature resistance. Both types of polymers are essential in the flame-retardant thermoplastic market for various industrial applications.</p></p>
