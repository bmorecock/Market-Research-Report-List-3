<p><p>Leaded resistors can be categorized into three main types based on their composition: carbon film resistors, metal film resistors, and metal oxide resistors. Carbon film resistors are made by depositing a carbon film on a ceramic rod, providing stable performance at a low cost. Metal film resistors use a thin metal layer to achieve high precision and low noise levels. Metal oxide resistors have a metal oxide film for high power handling capabilities. Each type of leaded resistor has its own unique characteristics and applications in various electronic devices.</p></p>
