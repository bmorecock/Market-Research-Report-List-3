<p><p>An Implanted Venous Access Device, also known as a Port-a-Cath or Port, is a small medical device implanted under the skin that provides easy access to a patient's veins for the administration of medication, fluids, blood products, and for blood sampling. These devices are commonly used for patients undergoing chemotherapy, long-term antibiotic therapy, frequent blood transfusions, or for those with difficult veins.</p><p>The Implanted Venous Access Device Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.90% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases such as cancer, kidney failure, and infectious diseases, which require long-term intravenous therapy. Additionally, advancements in technology leading to improved device design, materials, and safety features are driving the market growth.</p><p>The market is also witnessing a trend towards the development of minimally invasive procedures for implanting these devices, reducing the risk of infection and improving patient comfort. Furthermore, increasing healthcare expenditure, growing demand for personalized medicine, and rising awareness about the benefits of implanted venous access devices are contributing to the market expansion. Overall, the future looks promising for the Implanted Venous Access Device Market as the demand for efficient and reliable access to veins continues to rise.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>PICC</li><li>Centrally Inserted Catheters (non-tunnelled and tunnelled)</li><li>Implanted Ports</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Implanted Venous Access Device market includes various types such as Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC), Centrally Inserted Catheters (including non-tunnelled and tunnelled options), Implanted Ports, and other devices. PICCs are inserted into the arm for long-term access. Centrally Inserted Catheters are placed directly into a major vein, either with or without a tunnel under the skin. Implanted Ports are placed under the skin and accessed by a needle. Other types of devices may include tunneled catheters or hybrid devices.</p></p>
