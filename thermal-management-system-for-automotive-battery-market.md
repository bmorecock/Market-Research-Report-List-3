<p><p>The thermal management system for automotive batteries includes three main types: liquid cooling, air cooling, and refrigerant cooling. </p><p>Liquid cooling involves circulating a coolant through a series of tubes to regulate the temperature of the battery. </p><p>Air cooling uses fans or vents to draw cool air over the battery to dissipate heat. </p><p>Refrigerant cooling utilizes a refrigeration cycle to remove heat from the battery. </p><p>Each type of cooling system has its own advantages and is used in different automotive applications based on specific requirements and preferences.</p></p>
