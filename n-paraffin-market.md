<p><p>N-Paraffin is a type of hydrocarbon compound that is commonly divided into three main market types based on their carbon chain length. Below C10 N-Paraffin includes chains of carbon atoms less than 10, C10-C13 N-Paraffin consists of chains between 10 and 13 carbon atoms, and C14 and Above N-Paraffin includes chains with 14 or more carbon atoms. Each market type has unique applications and characteristics, making them suitable for various industrial uses such as lubricants, solvents, and waxes.</p></p>
