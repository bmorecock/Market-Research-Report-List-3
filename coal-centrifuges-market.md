<p><p>Coal centrifuges are used in the coal industry to separate coal particles based on their size. Coarse coal centrifuges are designed to handle larger coal particles, typically ranging from 6 to 0.5 mm in size. On the other hand, fine coal centrifuges are used for smaller coal particles, ranging from 1 mm down to 0.1 mm. Both types of centrifuges play a crucial role in coal processing plants by helping to efficiently separate coal particles of different sizes, improving overall coal quality and maximizing coal recovery.</p></p>
