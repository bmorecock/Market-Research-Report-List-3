<p><ul><li>Physical Separation (Mechanical Methods, etc.)</li><li>Chemical Separation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Industrial oil water separators are classified into two main types: physical separation and chemical separation. Physical separation involves mechanical methods such as gravity separation, coalescence, and filtration, which rely on differences in density or size to separate oil and water. Chemical separation, on the other hand, involves the use of chemicals such as coagulants and flocculants to facilitate the separation process. Both types of separators are widely used in various industries to effectively remove oil from water for environmental and regulatory compliance purposes.</p></p>
